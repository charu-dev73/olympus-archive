HISTORY & MYTH



Greek mythology was more than religion. It was an attempt to understand existence itself — explaining love, revenge, suffering, war, pride, destiny, and mortality.




Across centuries, these myths shaped philosophy, literature, theatre, and the cultural identity of the ancient world.













THE LEGACY OF OLYMPUS



The Olympian gods reflected the virtues and flaws of humanity itself. They loved, envied, deceived, punished, desired, and destroyed.




Their stories endured because they revealed timeless truths about human nature, mortality, and the struggle against fate.
